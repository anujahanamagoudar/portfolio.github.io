Business Case: Student Admission Management

The Student Admission Management system aligns with the following Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs):

  • SDG 4: Quality Education

    Ensures inclusive and equitable quality education and promotes lifelong learning opportunities for all through a streamlined and accessible admission process.

  • SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities

    Promotes fairness and transparency in the admission process, reducing biases and ensuring equal opportunities for students from diverse backgrounds.

Implementation by using Hash Tables, Priority Queue (Min-heap or Max-heap), and Graphs (Dijkstra's Algorithm).

1. Graphs (Dijkstra's Algorithm)

Use Case: Seat allocation based on preferences and availability.

  • Represent students and institutions as nodes.
  • Connect a student node to preferred institutions with edges.
  • Weight the edges based on preference rankings or eligibility criteria.

Advantages:

  • Quickly identifies the best institution for a student based on weighted preferences (e.g., proximity, scores).
  • Handles large datasets efficiently with priority queues.

2. Priority Queue

Use Case: Process applications based on priority (e.g., merit, reservation, or first-come-first-serve).

  • Priority: Merit scores or time of application.
  • Data Structure: Min-heap or Max-heap.

Advantages:

  • Guarantees the highest-priority application is processed first.
  • Efficient insertion and extraction (O(log n)).

3. Hash Table

Use Case: Efficiently store and retrieve student records.

  • Key: Student ID or unique application number.
  • Value: Details like name, scores, preferences, and status.

Advantages:

  • Fast lookups for student data (O(1) on average).
  • Easily update records during the admission process.

Business Case: Feedback and Quality Assurance

1. Hash Tables

A Hash Map can be used to store and efficiently retrieve feedback data, associating unique identifiers (e.g., student IDs) with their feedback or performance data. This allows for fast updates and retrieval of feedback for specific individuals.

Use Cases:

  • Feedback Collection and Storage: Use a hash map to store feedback scores indexed by unique IDs, such as student or employee IDs.

2. AVL Trees

AVL Trees are self-balancing binary search trees that provide fast insertion, deletion, and lookup operations while maintaining a balanced structure. This is useful for maintaining sorted feedback scores, quality metrics, or performance evaluations.

Use Cases:

  • Feedback Sorted by Scores: Store feedback scores sorted by score to quickly identify top performers and evaluate quality trends.

3. Priority Queue

A Priority Queue is a data structure where elements are assigned priorities. It can be useful in feedback processing, such as handling higher priority feedback first (e.g., negative feedback).

Use Cases:

  • Processing Feedback by Priority: Use a priority queue to process feedback based on urgency or rating score.
