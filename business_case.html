Business Cases Implementation

Business Case: Student Admission Management

The Student Admission Management system aligns with the following Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs):

  • SDG 4: Quality Education

    Ensures inclusive and equitable quality education and promotes lifelong learning opportunities for all through a streamlined and accessible admission process.

  • SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities

    Promotes fairness and transparency in the admission process, reducing biases and ensuring equal opportunities for students from diverse backgrounds.

Implementation by using Hash Tables, Priority Queue (Min-heap or Max-heap).

1. Priority Queue

Use Case: Streamline the application process by processing applications based on merit score and document verification.

Example Implementation:

  • Priority: Higher merit scores are prioritized for processing, and applications with verified documents are eligible for seat allocation.
  • Data Structure: Max-heap implemented using priority_queue.

Advantages:

  • Ensures fairness by processing high-priority applications first.
  • Efficient handling of insertion and extraction operations (O(log n) complexity).

2. Hash Table

Use Case: Efficiently store, retrieve, and update student application records during the admission process.

Example Implementation:

  • Key: Unique Student ID.
  • Value: Details such as name, merit score, preferences, document status, and seat allocation status.

Advantages:

  • Quickly access and manage student data (O(1) average complexity for lookups).
  • Facilitates seamless updates to records during document verification and seat allocation stages.
